## Brand Voice & Tone

### Core Voice Characteristics

**Warm but Authoritative**
- Approachable, not clinical
- Confident, not arrogant
- Evidence-based, not woo-woo

**Premium but Accessible**
- High-end without being elitist
- Exclusive without being exclusionary
- Sophisticated without being pretentious

**Social but Intentional**
- Community-focused, not party-focused
- Wellness-first, not trend-chasing
- Results-driven, not fad-driven

### Tone by Section

| Section | Tone | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| **Hero** | Inspiring, aspirational | "Where longevity meets community" |
| **Experiences** | Educational, benefit-focused | "Accelerate recovery in 60 minutes" |
| **Memberships** | Value-driven, clear | "4 credits monthly, unlimited ice baths" |
| **Events** | Professional, exciting | "Transform your celebration into recovery" |
| **FAQ** | Helpful, reassuring | "Yes, you can upgrade anytime after 3 months" |

### Voice Do's ✅

- Use "you" and "your" (direct address)
- Use active voice ("You'll experience..." not "Experiences will be had...")
- Be specific with numbers and outcomes
- Use power words: transform, accelerate, unlock, optimize, restore
- Include emotional triggers: feel, experience, discover, achieve

### Voice Don'ts ❌

- Avoid vague wellness speak ("unlock your inner glow")
- Avoid clichés ("experience the future of wellness")
- Avoid passive voice ("it is designed to...")
- Avoid jargon without explanation
- Avoid excessive adjectives ("amazing, incredible, revolutionary")

## Grammar & Punctuation Standards

### Capitalization Rules

**Always Capitalize:**
- Service names: "HBOT Chamber," "Contrast Suite," "Red Light Therapy"
- Membership tiers: "The Restore," "The Ritual," "The Sanctuary"
- Proper nouns: "Breathe Wellness Sanctuary," "Phuket," "Thailand"

**Never Capitalize (unless start of sentence):**
- Generic terms: "sauna," "ice bath," "cold plunge"
- Common nouns: "membership," "experience," "credit"
- Prepositions in headlines: "Self-Care Made Social" (not "Made Social")

**Consistent Terms:**
- ✅ "Tech-Remedy" (singular) / "Tech-Remedies" (plural) — **Pick one and use consistently**
- ✅ "Ice Bath" (when referring to our specific service) / "ice bath" (generic)
- ✅ "Contrast Therapy" (proper name) / "contrast therapy" (generic practice)

### Punctuation

**Em Dashes (—)**
- Use for emphasis or interruption: "Recovery—faster than ever"
- Use instead of parentheses for asides

**En Dashes (–)**
- Use for ranges: "$395–$1,195 per month"
- Use for connections: "Sauna–Cold–Rest cycle"

**Commas**
- Use Oxford comma in lists: "Sauna, cold plunge, and red light therapy"
- Use before "and" in compound sentences

**Apostrophes**
- "Memberships" (plural) not "Membership's"
- "Guest passes" not "Guest pass's"

### Article Usage

**Always include articles:**
- ✅ "Book an Experience" (not "Book Experience")
- ✅ "Join the Sanctuary" (not "Join Sanctuary")
- ✅ "Schedule a Tour" (not "Schedule Tour")

**Exception:** Short CTAs can drop articles if space-constrained
- "Book Now" (acceptable)
- "Join Now" (acceptable)

## CRO Principles

### 1. Benefit-First Language

**Formula:** Outcome + Timeframe + Proof

**Bad (Feature-Focused):**
> "Our HBOT chamber operates at 1.5 ATA pressure."

**Good (Benefit-Focused):**
> "Accelerate recovery in 60 minutes. Our HBOT chamber delivers 10x more oxygen to your cells, reducing inflammation and speeding muscle repair."

### 2. Specificity Over Vague Claims

**Bad:**
> "Feel better than ever"
> "Transform your wellness"
> "Unlock your potential"

**Good:**
> "Reduce muscle soreness by 40% in 24 hours"
> "Increase energy levels for 48 hours post-session"
> "Join 2,500+ members who've transformed their recovery routine"

### 3. Social Proof Integration

**Always include:**
- Specific numbers: "2,500+ members" (not "thousands")
- Timeframes: "Since 2023" or "In the last 6 months"
- Credibility markers: "4.9/5 rating" with review count
- Expert validation: "Recommended by [Name], [Credential]"

### 4. Urgency & Scarcity (When Appropriate)

**Use sparingly and truthfully:**
- ✅ "Limited to 200 members per location"
- ✅ "Only 3 spots remaining this week"
- ❌ "Limited time offer" (if not time-limited)
- ❌ "Only today" (if always available)

### 5. Risk Reversal

**Include where relevant:**
- "Upgrade or downgrade anytime after 3 months"
- "Credits rollover month-to-month"
- "Cancel anytime with 30 days notice"
- "100% satisfaction guarantee" (if true)

**Formula 1: Benefit + Differentiator**
> "Self-Care Made Social — Where Longevity Meets Community"

> "Recover Faster. Perform Better. Feel Stronger. — Science-Backed Wellness in Phuket"

> "Tired of Recovery Taking Days? — Experience 60-Minute Restoration"

**Formula 1: Action + Benefit**
> "Accelerate Recovery in Our Tech-Remedies"

**Formula 2: Question + Answer**

> "Reduce inflammation by 40% in 24 hours with our Contrast Therapy protocol"

## CTA Guidelines

### CTA Hierarchy

**Primary CTA (Most Important Action):**
- Action verb + specific outcome
- Examples:
  - "Book Your Experience"
  - "Join the Sanctuary"
  - "Schedule a Tour"
  - "Request a Proposal"

**Secondary CTA (Alternative Action):**
- Softer, exploratory language
- Examples:
  - "Explore Memberships"
  - "Learn More"
  - "View Experiences"
  - "See Pricing"

### CTA Best Practices

**Do:**
- ✅ Use action verbs: Book, Join, Schedule, Reserve, Start
- ✅ Be specific: "Book Your Experience" not "Click Here"
- ✅ Create urgency: "Book Now" (when space is limited)
- ✅ Match intent: "Join Now" for memberships, "Book Experience" for services

**Don't:**
- ❌ Use generic: "Submit," "Continue," "Next"
- ❌ Create false urgency: "Limited Time" (if not true)
- ❌ Be vague: "Get Started" (start what?)
- ❌ Use passive: "Experience can be booked"

### CTA Placement

**Above the fold:** Primary CTA visible without scrolling  
**Mid-page:** Secondary CTA or supporting action  
**End of section:** Clear next step  
**Sticky/fixed:** Primary CTA always accessible (mobile)

## Social Proof Standards

### Testimonial Format

**Structure:**
1. Specific outcome or benefit
2. Name and credential/location (if relevant)
3. Photo (if available)
4. Date (if recent)

**Example:**
> "Reduced my post-workout soreness by 50%. The Contrast Suite is now essential to my training."
> — **Sarah Chen**, Professional Triathlete, Phuket  
> *Member since January 2024*

### Statistics Format

**Always include:**
- Specific number (not "thousands")
- Timeframe (if relevant)
- Source (if external)

**Examples:**
- ✅ "4.9/5 rating from 127 verified reviews"
- ✅ "2,500+ active members across 3 locations"
- ✅ "98% member retention rate"
- ✅ "4,000+ experiences booked in 2024"

### Expert Validation

**Format:**
> "Recommended by [Name], [Title/Credential]"

**Example:**
> "Our HBOT protocols are designed in consultation with Dr. [Name], Board-Certified Hyperbaric Medicine Specialist"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### 1. Vague Wellness Speak

**Avoid:**
- "Unlock your inner glow"
- "Awaken your potential"
- "Transform your energy"
- "Elevate your vibration"

**Replace with:**
- "Increase collagen production by 30%"
- "Reduce inflammation markers in 48 hours"
- "Boost energy levels for 2-3 days"
- "Improve sleep quality by 40%"

### 2. Contradictory Language

**Avoid:**
- "An inclusive club with exclusive memberships" (contradictory)
- "Affordable luxury" (confusing positioning)

**Replace with:**
- "An inclusive community with thoughtfully limited memberships"
- "Premium wellness accessible through flexible membership tiers"

### 3. Passive Voice

**Avoid:**
- "Experiences are designed to..."
- "Memberships can be upgraded..."
- "Services are available..."

**Replace with:**
- "We design each experience to..."
- "You can upgrade your membership..."
- "You can book services..."

### 4. Missing Context

**Avoid:**
- "4 Credits" (what are they worth?)
- "Book Now" (book what?)
- "Join the Club" (what club?)

**Replace with:**
- "4 Tech-Remedy Credits monthly"
- "Book Your Experience"
- "Join Breathe Wellness Sanctuary"

### 5. Over-Promising

**Avoid:**
- "Guaranteed results"
- "Cure all ailments"
- "100% effective"

**Replace with:**
- "Clinically proven benefits"
- "Research-backed protocols"
- "Proven to reduce [specific metric]"

## Quick Reference Checklist

### Before Publishing Any Copy

**Grammar & Style:**
- [ ] All proper nouns capitalized consistently
- [ ] Articles included ("Book an Experience" not "Book Experience")
- [ ] Active voice used throughout
- [ ] No passive constructions
- [ ] Consistent terminology (check dictionary)

- [ ] Benefits stated before features
- [ ] Specific numbers included (not vague)

- [ ] Emotional triggers included
